#####
Tools
#####

Overview
========

This page lists tools, software and otherwise, that I find useful in my work
and hobbies. Prices shown for software are current at the time of writing and
taken from the respective distributors' websites. Some tools have discounted
upgrade pricing and/or higher or lower pricing for different product versions
and feature sets.

Software Tools
==============

The Bat!
********

The Bat! from Ritlabs, SRL is a great Windows email application for the
paranoid. They also distribute a portable version, "The Bat! Voyager," and an
SMTP server, "Batpost."

You can get The Bat! here: `The Bat! <https://www.ritlabs.com/en/products/thebat/>`_

Price: $59.99

Beyond Compare
**************

Beyond Compare is a great portable, cross-platform comparison tool from Scooter
Software. You can use it to compare text, files, folders, images, etc.. It can
be integrated with your SCM of choice, e.g., Git, Perforce, Mercurial, etc..

You can get Beyond Compare here: `Beyond Compare <https://www.scootersoftware.com/>`_

Price: $60

Cygwin
******

Great for getting a \*NIX feel on Windows. The first time I used Cygwin was in
college, and it briefly ruined my life. I have since come to love it, and it is
sometimes necessary for building software on Windows.

You can get Cygwin here: `Cygwin <https://cygwin.com/>`_

Price: Free

dBpoweramp
**********

Essential CD ripper and audio conversion tool from illustrate. I'm not a fan of
their PerfectTunes software, but the dBpoweramp CD Ripper and Music Converter
are the best. This is an example of software that keeps me on Windows.

You can get dBpoweramp here: `dBpoweramp <https://www.dbpoweramp.com/>`_

Price: $68

D4 Time
*******

Are any of your clocks correct? It may be surprising to many people to learn
how poorly their PC clocks keep time. Dimension 4 Time from Thinking Man
Software periodically forces an NTP sync between your Windows PC and various
NTP servers across the world. You can also configure D4 Time to sync against
local or private NTP servers you have access to. It keeps your PC clock more
accurate than your local AD domain controller.

You can get D4 Time here: `D4 Time <http://www.thinkman.com/dimension4/>`_

Price: Free for personal use; $10 for commercial use.

IntelliJ IDEA
*************

Java IDE from JetBrains. This is my go-to for Java and Scala. I recommend the
all-products pack from JetBrains, because their other IDEs and tools are also
great. If you program in C#, definitely check out ReSharper.

You can get IntelliJ IDEA here: `IntelliJ IDEA <https://www.jetbrains.com/idea/>`_

Price: $249

KeePass
*******

A great password manager for .NET/Mono. I use this on Windows. The last time I
tried, it didn't work well on macOS or Linux, but Mono has probably come a long
way since then.

You can get KeePass here: `KeePass <https://keepass.info/>`_

Price: Free

Macrium Reflect
***************

I don't completely trust cloud backup solutions like Code42 CrashPlan or
Backblaze to be my only backup of my important data. I like to have a local
snapshot of the complete state of my machines for quick recovery and retrieval
of my data. I use Macruim Reflect on all my Windows machines to take daily
snapshots of my data. In the event of a hardware failure, I can quickly mount
snapshot images as though they were locally-attached hard drives and retrieve
data from them. You can also completely restore a machine from backup to a new
hard drive if your HDD or SSD fails and is replaced.

You can get Macrium Reflect here: `Macrium Reflect <https://www.macrium.com/products/home>`_

Price: $69.95 for a single machine; $139.95 for a "family" four-pack.

MobaXterm
*********

I like MobaXterm for keeping track of SSH sessions on Windows.

You can get MobaXterm here: `MobaXterm <https://mobaxterm.mobatek.net/>`_

Price: $69

Notepad++
*********

A great free text editor for Windows. I use Vim on \*NIX and Notepad++ on
Windows.

You can get Notepad++ here: `Notepad++ <https://notepad-plus-plus.org/>`_

Price: Free

Plex
****

I run a home media server using Plex on a Synology NAS unit. The great thing
about it is that everyone in the household can have his or her own profile for
keeping track of what they've watched, playlists, etc., and we can restrict
what the kids can see without any risk of them being exposed to harmful
advertising.

We use Plex to store and serve up our movies, TV shows, music, and audiobooks.
We also use Plex in combination with an HDHomeRun DTV tuner to act as a DVR for
live TV and movies. It's the same number of OTA ads without the cost of a pay
TV subscription.

I recommend purchasing the Plex Pass Lifetime subscription.

You can get Plex here: `Plex <https://www.plex.tv/>`_

Price: $119.99

Sizer
*****

Sizer from Brian Apps (no affiliation) lets you specify exact pixel count sizes
for your Windows windows. I recommend the version 4.0 preview over the older
32-bit distribution.

You can get Sizer 4.0 here: `Sizer 4.0 <http://www.brianapps.net/sizer4/>`_

Price: Free

VMware Workstation
******************

It's better than VirtualBox and has a ton of features. I use VMware Workstation
Pro for my local development VMs.

You can get VMware Workstation here: `VMware Workstation <https://www.vmware.com/products/workstation-pro.html>`_

Price: $199

X1 Search
*********

If you archive your email, e.g. from Outlook or Gmail, X1 Search from 
X1 Discovery, Inc. allows you to "search at the speed of thought." You can also
configure it to index your local files and other data sources for super-fast
searching. Better than ``grep -R`` or ``find /``. If you run a large
business, the company also offers enterprise-grade solutions for searching
across many machines and data repositories.

You can get X1 Search here: `X1 Search <https://www.x1.com/products/x1-search/>`_

Price: $79
